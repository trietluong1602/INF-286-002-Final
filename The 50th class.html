Introducing the Great   

A ccording to the cover of our 1960 Spindrift, we was supposed to be Oceanside's 50 th high school graduating class* of  Sailors, even though our school building was less than five years old when we were graduated. And our great class was 43% larger than the previous recordbreaking class of 1959. The name of all of our graduates are listed below. (Those who were members of our class earlier but were not among the June 1960 graduates are not listed below.)

Interestingly, our class also claimed the distinction of having an unusually high number of twins, 8 pairs (16 twins) out of 500+ graduates. Can you identify all the twins? 

______________

* 

We can neither explain nor verify this since the first class (consisting of only two people) was graduated in 1903 (57 years before ours), and consistent with that, 47 and 52 years later, the classes of 2007 and 2012 purported to be the 104th and 109 th class, respectively, as if there was a graduating class every year. which would make ours the 57th class. Go figure.
